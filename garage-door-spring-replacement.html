Signs You Need It

How to know your garage door spring is broken

Springs are under enormous tension. When one breaks, the symptoms are immediate and obvious — do not try to lift the door manually until it has been inspected.

Loud snap or bang

A spring failure usually announces itself with a sharp metallic sound, often when no one is using the door. That noise is the spring releasing its stored tension.

Door will not lift

The opener hums or struggles, but the door barely moves or stops a few inches up. The opener is not strong enough to lift the door without the spring carrying the weight.

Visible gap in the spring

Look up at the torsion spring above the door. If you see a clear gap or two separated pieces, the spring has failed.

Door slams down hard

If the door closes faster than normal or lands with force, the spring is no longer counterbalancing the weight properly. Stop using the door and call.

Cables hanging loose

Loose or detached cables often follow a spring break. The whole lift system depends on spring tension to keep the cables taut.

Crooked door

One side higher than the other usually means a single spring on a two-spring system has failed and the remaining spring is doing all the work.

Safety

Why spring replacement is not a DIY job

Garage door springs hold hundreds of pounds of stored tension. A spring that releases unexpectedly during installation can cause serious injury. We use the right winding bars, proper torque measurements, and balance verification to keep the work safe.

Pairs

Should both springs be replaced together?

If your door has two springs and one breaks, the other is usually near the end of its life as well. Replacing both at once avoids a second service call within months and keeps tension balanced. We will give you the honest call based on what we see.
