## 2. Review Generation Strategy

### Target: 25+ Google Reviews Within 4-6 Months

**Current state**: 5.0 rating, very low count. The goal is to build volume while maintaining 4.8+ average.

### System: Post-Job Review Request Workflow

**Step 1 - Same day (in-person)**
- After completing the job and confirming the customer is satisfied, Eyal says:
  > "I really appreciate your business. If you're happy with the work, a Google review helps other homeowners in [neighborhood] find us. I can text you the link right now if that's easier."
- This direct, in-person ask while goodwill is highest converts at 50-70%

**Step 2 - Text message (same day, within 2 hours)**
- Send a short SMS with the direct Google review link:
  > "Hi [Name], thanks for choosing Springs Garage Door Services today. If you have a minute, a quick Google review really helps: [direct review link]. Thank you! - Eyal"
- Use the GBP short review link from the GBP dashboard (Share > "Ask for reviews")

**Step 3 - Follow-up (3 days later, if no review)**
- One follow-up text or email:
  > "Hi [Name], just checking that your garage door is working great after the [service type]. If everything looks good, I'd appreciate a quick review here: [link]. Thanks again!"
- No more than one follow-up. Never pressure.

### Review Link Distribution

- **Primary**: Direct Google review link via text message (highest conversion)
- **Secondary**: QR code on a printed "Thank You" card left at the job site
- **Tertiary**: Email signature with "Review us on Google" link
- **Website**: Add a "Leave a Review" link/button to the site footer and thank-you/confirmation page

### Review Volume Targets

| Month | Cumulative Reviews | Pace |
|-------|-------------------|------|
| Month 1 | 5-8 | Ask every customer, aim for 2/week |
| Month 2 | 10-15 | Maintain momentum |
| Month 3 | 18-22 | Start seeing Map Pack ranking improvements |
| Month 4-6 | 25-35 | Sustainable 2-3/month ongoing |

### Review Response Protocol

- **Respond to every review within 24 hours** (positive and negative)
- Positive review response template:
  > "Thank you, [Name]. It was great working on your [service type] in [area]. Glad the door is running well. We're here anytime you need anything."
- Negative review response:
  > "Thank you for the feedback, [Name]. I'd like to understand what happened and make it right. Please call me directly at 719-308-9951 so we can discuss this."
- Include service keywords and location names naturally in responses (this helps local SEO)
- Never argue. Take the conversation offline.

### Diversify Across Platforms

- After reaching 25+ on Google, start steering some customers to Yelp and Facebook
- Nextdoor recommendations happen organically when neighbors ask for referrals
- Never incentivize reviews with discounts or payments (violates platform policies)

## 3. Content Pillars for Social Media

### Pillar 1: Job Stories (40% of content)
Before/after transformations, completed projects, problem-to-solution narratives.
- Before/after photo pairs or carousels
- "What was wrong and how we fixed it" mini case studies
- New installation showcases with door style and brand details
- Opener upgrades and smart-home integration stories

**Why it works**: Visual proof of competence. Homeowners scroll and think "I want my garage to look like that."

### Pillar 2: Trust & Expertise (25% of content)
Educational content that positions Eyal as the knowledgeable local expert.
- Seasonal maintenance tips ("5 things to check before winter")
- Safety warnings ("Why you should never DIY a torsion spring replacement")
- "How to tell if your garage door needs repair vs. replacement"
- Product comparisons and brand recommendations
- Quick explainer videos (60-90 seconds)

**Why it works**: Builds authority. When a spring breaks at 6am, the homeowner remembers the guy who posted the helpful video.

### Pillar 3: Local & Community (20% of content)
Hyper-local content that connects the business to Colorado Springs life.
- "Just finished a job in [Monument/Falcon/Black Forest]" with location tag
- Colorado Springs weather-related tips (hail season, freeze prep)
- Shout-outs to other local businesses and referral partners
- Community event mentions (local festivals, charity, school events)
- Nextdoor-style neighborhood engagement

**Why it works**: Signals "I'm your neighbor, not a call center." Local content gets shared by local people.

### Pillar 4: Behind the Scenes / Owner Story (15% of content)
Humanize the brand and differentiate from corporate competitors.
- Eyal on the job (truck loading, arriving at a site, inspecting a door)
- Day-in-the-life clips
- "Why I started Springs Garage Door Services"
- Responding to funny or unusual garage door situations
- Milestone posts (job count, years in business, new equipment)

**Why it works**: People hire people, not logos. This is the biggest advantage a small owner-led business has over national chains.

## 5. Platform-Specific Tactics
