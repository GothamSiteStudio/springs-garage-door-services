What We Repair

Common garage door repairs in Colorado Springs

Most repair calls fall into a handful of categories. The diagnosis determines whether the right fix is a quick adjustment, a hardware replacement, or a deeper rebuild.

Broken springs

A snapped torsion or extension spring is the single most common reason a garage door stops lifting. Repair includes safe replacement, balance check, and verification that the opener is not strained. More on spring replacement →

Off-track doors

A door that has jumped its track is unsafe to operate. We realign the rollers, inspect the track and brackets for damage, and confirm balance before putting the door back into service.

Opener failures

Motors that hum without lifting, remotes that stop responding, sensors that misalign, and keypads that go dead. Most opener problems are quick fixes once the cause is identified. More on opener service →

Cable and drum issues

Frayed cables, slipped drums, or uneven lift on one side put the entire system at risk. Repair restores even tension and stops the issue from cascading into a larger failure.

Panel damage

Dented, cracked, or warped panels can often be replaced individually instead of forcing a full door replacement. We check fit, color match, and structural integrity before recommending the path.

Noisy operation

Squeaks, grinding, and rattling usually point to worn rollers, dry hinges, loose hardware, or an unbalanced door. A tune-up often resolves the noise and extends the door's working life.
